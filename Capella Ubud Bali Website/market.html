Market Segmentation

MARKET SEGMENTATION, DESCRIPTION & GUIDELINES

Each hotel reports Market Segments based on the same criteria and in accordance with the Accounting Standards in order to generate consistency in budgets, forecasts and P&L Statements.

The Director of Revenue Management is accountable for the correct segmentation.

1. Transient Market Segments

Less than 10 rooms, or as determined by the property

  • • Rack: Every rate that is available to the general public, directly to the end consumer which reflects the BAR rate and has no further discounts (only exception - Consortia Rate) or added value (except breakfast, halfboard or fullboard if the hotel offers rates that include a mealplan only or as a choice) regardless of the channel the rate is sold. Not timely limited or bound to special conditions.
    Example: BAR Rate, Consortia Rate (even if discounted), all Bookings from OTAs that are selling at BAR Rate directly to the end consumer without a markup (booking.com, etc.)
  • • Corporate: All Special Corporate Rates as outlined in your Sales and Marketing Plan.
  • • Volume: All Volume Account Rates as outlined in your Sales and Marketing Plan.
  • • RSVP: Only for hotels that run a RSVP program. All rates for RSVP Members, RSVP Referrals and all Special Promotions or Packages which are solely and exclusively distributed to RSVP Members.
  • • Discount / Promotion: Any rate that has a discount (either percentage, amount, value add or credit) off BAR (except Consortia such as Amex, Virtuoso), that is only available to a limited audience or is bound to special conditions such as prepayment, advanced booking or cancellation policies. If available to the general public without restrictions, the rate must be for a limited time and promoted by a separate Marketing Initiative.
    Example: Advance Purchase, Owners Rate, Long Term Stays, Early Booking Rate, Staff Rate, Friends and Family, LHW Worldwide Sale, Rakuten Super Sale, Summer Promotion, Winter Promotion, Special Rates given by Sales or General Manager.
  • • Package: Any rate that offers an additional item other than breakfast, halfboard, fullboard or credits. Package item can be a special meal plan (if outside halfboard and fullboard or if not already included in the hotels BAR rate), SPA items, activities, special events, tickets, etc. Packages outside special events should be available the whole year round.
    Example: Culinary Packages, Opera Package, Shopping Package, Christmas Package, New Year's Package.
  • • Wholesale: All rates that are sold net of commission/markup to a Wholesaler, Tour Operator, DMC or Merchant Model Online Channel where the markup is deducted upfront.
  • • Complimentary: All reservations given complimentary for sales reasons such as Barter, Site Inspection, Compensation or Business Contacts.
  • • House Use: All reservations given complimentary for non-sales reasons such as MOD room, Technicians, Staff room, Corporate Staff, etc. These bookings should not affect the occupancy or average daily rate.

2. Group Market Segments

More than 10 rooms or as determined by the hotel, on at least one of the stay dates within the stay period booked in the initial request.

  • Corporate Group:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that belong to a corporation or company and visit the hotel on business reasons such as meetings, conferences, exhibitions or incentives regardless if the meeting is in-house or at an outside location.
  • Association Group:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that belong to one or more Associations and visit the hotel for reasons such as meetings, conferences, exhibitions or incentives regardless if the meeting is in-house or at an outside location.
  • Citywide Group:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that visit and purpose of booking is a Citywide Event. Groups that stay during the period of a Citywide Event, but their purpose is not attending the Event, should not be considered Citywide Groups.
  • Entertainment Groups:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that belong to the Entertainment Business such as Music Groups, Film or TV-Crews, Celebrities, etc.
  • Government Groups:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that belong to a Department of the Government, Military or as part of a delegation.
  • Sports / Leisure Groups:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together for a Sport Event, such as Sport Teams (either professional, amateur or hobby) or Groups that are visiting for Wellness, Relaxation or Sightseeing such as Tour Operator Guided Groups or Individual Groups.
  • Social / Other Group:
    Groups (rooms only or with function space) booked and travelling together that visit the hotel for a Social Event such as Weddings, Anniversaries, Graduation Events, Bar Mitzwas, Awards Shows, etc. regardless if the event is in-house or at an outside location. This Market Segment is also used for any Groups, which do not fit into one of the above mentioned scenarios.

3. Contract Rooms

Year round consecutive bookings.

Example: Airline Crews, etc.
