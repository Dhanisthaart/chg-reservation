Security Protocol

HOTEL BLACKLIST

Definition

A 'Blacklist' refers to a list of guests who have engaged in unacceptable or unethical behaviour and/or activities at the hotel. There must be a reasonable and non-arbitrary reason for the guest to be placed on Blacklist. The property's General Manager must endorse the Blacklisted Guest and highlight the profile to Corporate Office within 24 hours.

Once a guest has been placed on Blacklist, they will automatically be blacklisted from all hotels and resorts managed by Capella Hotel Group (CHG). All properties under CHG reserve the rights to not accommodate check-in and/or any service requests of the aforementioned.

Conditions for Blacklist

A Guest can be placed on the hotel's blacklist based on the following condition:

  • Engaged in fraudulence
  • Performed verbal abuse and/or threatened Colleagues/Teammates
  • Assaulted / physically abused Colleagues/Teammates and/or other guests
  • Caused problems and/or significant property damages
  • Engaged in illegal behaviour within the hotel's premise
  • Sexual harassment towards Colleagues/Teammates and/or other guests

Banned Actions

Profiles on this list are banned from:

  • Making a room reservation and checking-in to the hotel/resort
  • Making a dining reservation at all food and beverage outlets within the hotel
  • Making a booking or utilizing any hotel facilities, amenities and services

If the property decides to remove the guest from Blacklist, the General Manager must notify the Senior Vice President of Operations prior to proceeding with the necessary action to update the guest's profile. No guest may be removed from blacklist without written endorsement from both the property GM and SVP Operations. Corporate Office must update Salesforce and OPERA records accordingly.

Confidentiality & Record Keeping

  • All blacklist records must be handled confidentially and used strictly for operational purposes.
  • Access to blacklisted profiles is restricted to authorized users only.
  • Supporting documentation such as CCTV footage, colleague statements, or incident reports must be retained for a minimum of 3 years (or longer, based on local regulations).

Steps to Placing an in-house Guest on Blacklist

  • Inform your manager when the guest misbehaved.
  • Should the guest not cooperate, inform Security to escort them to a quiet area/seat.
  • Notify the General Manager and the General Manager must meet the guest to explain the reason for the blacklist.
  • General Manager to send a formal email communication to the guest, stating their discussion. (The communication must remain professional, factual, and non-emotional, stating observed behaviours and consequences.)
  • In the event where guest is intoxicated, inform Security, escort the guest back to their room and have the General Manager to meet the guest the following day.
  • Raise a Glitch on Knowcross, under glitch description state the reason for the blacklist. An attachment of a post meeting email communication between the guest and General Manager must be updated in Glitch.
  • Update the VIP Level of Guest Profile on OPERA PMS to 'VIP 6 - Blacklist'
  • Update Background Notes on the top line with the following:
    BLACKLISTED PROFILE BY [PROPERTY NAME] ENDORSED BY [NAME OF GM], GENERAL MANAGER [COLLEAGUE'S/TEAMMATES'S SIGN-OFF]
  • Upload a photo of the guest in their Salesforce "Hapi Guest Focus" Profile
  • Ensure a formal Incident Report is logged, endorsed by Security, and attached to the guest record.

Steps to Placing a City Guest (Visitor) on Blacklist

  • Inform your manager when the guest misbehaved.
  • Politely explain that you are unable serve them in due to their ill conduct.
  • Should the guest not cooperate, inform Security to escort them to a quiet area/seat.
  • Notify the General Manager and the General Manager must meet the guest to explain the reason for the blacklist.
  • General Manager to send a formal email communication to the guest, stating their discussion
  • In the event where guest is intoxicated, inform Security, escort the guest out of the hotel/resort premise.
  • Raise a Visitor Glitch on Knowcross, under glitch description state the reason for the blacklist. An attachment of a post meeting email communication between the guest and General Manager must be updated in Glitch.
  • Create a Guest Profile on OPERA PMS and input VIP level as 'VIP 6 - Blacklist'. This serves as an official record.
  • Update Background Notes on the top line with the following:
    BLACKLISTED PROFILE BY [PROPERTY NAME] ENDORSED BY [NAME OF GM], GENERAL MANAGER [COLLEAGUE'S/TEAMMATES'S SIGN-OFF]
  • Under the same note and copy the description of glitch at the bottom for future reference.
  • Ensure a formal Incident Report is logged, endorsed by Security, and attached to the visitor profile.

All details to be provided to the following personnel within 24 hours of blacklist issuance:

  • Senior Vice President, Operations – Clive Edwards: Clive.Edwards@capellahotelgroup.com
  • Corporate Director, Security & Risk - Jonathan Pattenden: jonathan.pattenden@capellahotels.com
  • Senior Manager, Quality – Elaine Khoo: Elaine.Khoo@capellahotelgroup.com

Steps to Refusing a Blacklisted Guest from Checking-in

  • Upon guest's attempt to check-in, validate their personal identification and details against the reservation profile in OPERA
  • Do a profile look-up on Salesforce "Hapi Guest Focus", under the 'Search Tab' search "VIP6" to ascertain guest's identification. Select "View More" for the full list.
  • Inform your manager
  • Politely explained to the guest that you are unable to check them in due to previous incident
  • Should the guest not cooperate, inform Security. If guest becomes aggressive or physically threatening, Security must escalate to local authorities/police immediately.
  • Update the following information under OPERA Background Note on the second line:
    [Date (DD-MMM-YYYY), Time (HH:MM hrs): Guest attempted to check-in at [Property Name] [Colleague's/Teammate's sign off]

Escalation & Safety

  • If medical condition or mental health concerns are suspected, Security must prioritize guest's safety and involve local authorities if required.
  • Intoxicated guests must be managed discreetly, ensuring no risk to themselves, colleagues, or other guests.

Corporate Office Role & Group-Wide Consistency

  • Once a guest is blacklisted, Corporate Office will circulate the record to all CHG properties to ensure consistent enforcement across all hotels.
  • Properties must not override or bypass blacklist restrictions.
