Reservations Excellence Program - ASPIRE

Capella Hotels Group commitment to excellence is undisputable and we are continuously looking for ways to improve our products, services or processed because we believe continuous improvement process is key.

Reservations Excellence Program is a simple process for use to ensure just that. It is a Reservations Test Call program which will enable us to:

  • Maximized Conversions - While we pay a lot of focus on website conversions, same amount of effort should be spent on our phone conversions.
  • Branding - Represents the image of the hotel. Set guests expectation of the perceived level of service provided.
  • Improving Efficiency - Quality assurance. Review processes, service enhancement and training identification.
  • Optimized Revenue - Upsell opportunity. Drive incremental revenue.

Reservations test calls will be conducted by ASPIRE. Minimum 2 calls will be conducted per month per property. Standards used are Forbes and LQA customised with other requirement by CHG such as the questions on Capella Discovery Loyalty membership.

1. Answer within 3 rings or 10 seconds

What: Answer promptly with a warm, branded greeting and clear self‑identification.

Why: First impressions shape perceived luxury and reduce call abandonment.

How: Smile before answering, use full brand signature and offer assistance immediately.

Example: "Good morning. Thank you for calling (property), this is Dayu from reservations, how may I assist you?"

2. Obtain caller's name and use it 3 times

What: Politely ask how to address the caller; use their name naturally at least three times.

Why: Personalization builds rapport and confidence.

How: Ask early, use name 3-5 times naturally, confirm spelling once.

Example: "May I know who I am speaking with? Thank you Ms. Smith."

3. Calm and clear conversation, use headset

What: Deliver noise‑free, articulate conversation at measured pace.

Why: Audio quality reduces errors and supports luxury tone.

How: Mute notifications, minimize background noise.

Example: "Well noted. Please give me a second to record your details."

4. Ask permission before hold

What: Seek consent, give reason, set expectation before holding.

Why: Respect reduces frustration and keeps guest informed.

How: Ask permission + state purpose + give estimated time.

Example: "Mr. Smith, may I place you on a brief hold while I check availability?"

5. No hold longer than 30 seconds without call-back

What: Return at ~30 seconds and offer call-back option.

Why: Respects guest's time and prevents perceived neglect.

How: Return with options: continue holding or call-back.

Example: "This is taking longer. Would you prefer I call you back within five minutes?"

6. Clarify if guest stayed before

What: Determine first-time vs returning and tailor recognition.

Why: Recognition is hallmark of ultra-luxury and increases loyalty.

How: Ask early, offer warm welcome or welcome back.

First time: "Thank you for choosing our hotel!"
Returning: "We would be happy to welcome you back!"

7. Offer alternatives if unavailable

What: Present solvable options (alternate dates/room types).

Why: Keeps momentum and salvages conversion.

How: Offer 2-3 alternate options, always provide alternative.

Example: "While requested dates are full, I can offer [alt dates] or [alternative room]."

8. Ask open-ended question

What: Understand purpose, occasion, and success criteria.

Why: Relevant curation increases satisfaction and upsell.

How: Ask 1-2 open-ended questions, echo key details.

Example: "How can we make this anniversary most memorable for you?"

9. Transitional phrase reinforcing you can help

What: Reassure guest that you can and will help.

Why: Builds confidence and emotional ease.

How: Use sincere, polite transitions to signal ownership.

Example: "It would be my pleasure to see what we have available."

10. Hotel introduction and recommendations

What: Describe facilities concisely and recommend what fits needs.

Why: Demonstrates expertise and value.

How: Map features to needs, for return guests highlight updates.

Example: "All rates include: 24hrs Essentialist service, complimentary mini bar."

11. Clarify personal preferences

What: Capture bed, view, dietary, accessibility preferences.

Why: Enables anticipatory service and seamless planning.

How: Ask open questions, record precisely in profile.

Example: "Do you have any preferences or dietary restrictions we should know?"

12. Present 2 choices of room types before rates

What: Offer best-fit and elevated alternative; lead with benefits.

Why: Guests buy benefits; increases conversion and satisfaction.

How: Curate two strong options, use emotive benefit language.

Example: "For your honeymoon, we recommend Deluxe Double or Junior Suite King."

13. Describe physical elements by room type

What: Translate features into outcomes (space, privacy, views).

Why: Enhances perceived value and sets expectations.

How: Use accurate size/feature details, tie to guest's purpose.

Example: "Deluxe King: 50sqm, city view, luxury bathroom with tub and rainfall shower."

14. Inquire about DISCOVERY membership before rate quote

What: Check DISCOVERY status before quoting; recognize members.

Why: Drives recognition, value perception, and loyalty capture.

How: Ask early, if yes thank and offer member rate, if no offer enrollment.

Example: "We are glad to inform you that DISCOVERY Member rate is available with 10% OFF."

15. Present rate plan or package that meets needs

What: Match targeted package/promotion to needs with flexible backup.

Why: Increases relevance and revenue.

How: Recommend best-fit promotion + flexible alternative.

Example: "We have a special promotion. For flexible rate, we also have Best Available Rate."

16. Clearly state room rate with inclusions

What: Quote nightly rate, currency, taxes, and inclusions.

Why: Prevents confusion and future disputes.

How: State currency and ++, confirm what's included.

Example: "Daily rate is USD 500 + tax per night. Total for 3 nights is USD 1,650 including tax."

17. Confidently ask for sale

What: Close decisively and politely.

Why: Demonstrates professionalism and consultative salesmanship.

How: Ask directly to proceed. If no, offer alternatives.

Example: "Would you like me to proceed with the reservation?"

18. Obtain caller's contact details

What: Collect accurate contact points for confirmation.

Why: Ensures deliverability and continuity of service.

How: Ask for best contact number and email.

Example: "May I have your email address and phone number to send the confirmation?"

19. Explain deposit and cancellation policies

What: Outline deposit timing and cancellation penalties.

Why: Transparency builds trust and avoids disputes.

How: State deposit rules, clarify cancel-by date and penalties.

Example: "Free cancellation until 6pm local time, 2 days prior to arrival. Credit card required to guarantee."

20. Advise payment options

What: Offer accepted methods and capture guarantee.

Why: Convenience and security during booking.

How: Offer accepted card types, use secure capture.

Example: "May I have your credit card details to guarantee the reservation?"

21. Ascertain ETA, flight details, check-in time

What: Capture arrival time/flight; set check-in expectations.

Why: Enables smooth arrival and better room readiness.

How: Ask for ETA, flight details, offer transfers.

Example: "Do you have your flight details? Check-in is at 3pm, check-out at 12pm."

22. Provide transportation options with costs

What: Give clear transfer options with pricing; offer to arrange.

Why: Reduces travel stress and adds convenience.

How: Present hotel car with pricing, offer to book now.

Example: "May we assist with transportation? Return airport transfer is available at USD 150."

23. Offer hotel services (dinner, spa, programmes)

What: Recommend 1-2 relevant enhancements.

Why: Elevates stay and strengthens attachment to property.

How: Tie suggestions to occasion, offer to book.

Example: "Would you like me to arrange a dinner or spa reservation during your stay?"

24. Offer reservation confirmation number

What: Share confirmation number verbally and in email.

Why: Provides clear reference for follow-up.

How: Read clearly, invite guest to note it down.

Example: "Would you like to note your confirmation number? It is 12345678."

25. Advise pre-arrival email will be sent

What: Set expectation for personalized pre-arrival contact.

Why: Encourages engagement and reduces last-minute friction.

How: Advise timeline for confirmation and pre-arrival outreach.

Example: "Our Culturist will contact you closer to arrival for pre-arrival arrangements."

26. Repeat booking details for confirmation

What: Recap dates, room, rate, inclusions, payment, cancellation.

Why: Ensures accuracy and guest confidence.

How: Structure recap clearly, pause for corrections.

Example: "We have a booking from [date] to [date] in Deluxe room with total price..."

27. Thank guest and close with polite remark

What: End with sincere thanks, offer further help, restate name.

Why: Leaves strong final impression of care.

How: Ask if anything else needed, use guest's name, express anticipation.

Example: "Thank you Ms. Smith. We look forward to welcoming you."

28. Confirmation email is accurate

What: Email mirrors call recap precisely.

Why: Prevents disputes; reinforces trust.

How: Use approved template, proofread all details.

29. Confirmation within 2 hours

What: Written confirmation dispatched within two hours.

Why: Reduces anxiety and cements commitment.

How: Adhere to ≤2-hour, monitor pending confirmations.

Example: "Please expect your confirmation letter within 2 hours."

30. Guest fully prepared with positive anticipation

What: Call equips guest with clarity and positive anticipation.

Why: Drives satisfaction and reduces queries.

How: Provide clear next steps, key contacts, relevant updates.

Example: "Our team will conduct a courtesy call 14 days prior to your arrival."

31. Highly articulate, avoid slang

What: Use professional vocabulary and full sentences.

Why: Preserves ultra-luxury tone and clarity.

How: Replace slang with polished alternatives, avoid filler words.

Example: "Certainly, Mr. Smith. I will be pleased to check that for you."

32. Polite, gracious tone and pace

What: Maintain gentle cadence and empathetic tone.

Why: Creates emotional comfort and reduces friction.

How: Mirror guest's pace, use "please," "thank you," "my pleasure."

Example: "My pleasure, Mr. Smith. Thank you for your patience."

33. Genuine interest and concern

What: Acknowledge context and celebrate sentiments.

Why: Builds emotional loyalty beyond transaction.

How: Congratulate appropriately, demonstrate active listening.

Example: "Congratulations on your wedding! It's wonderful to have you here for your honeymoon."

34. Lead conversation, anticipatory service

What: Guide call with expertise; anticipate adjacent needs.

Why: Guests expect confident advisor who reduces decision effort.

How: Use summarize → recommend → confirm pattern.

Example: "Knowing your trip, we recommend Deluxe Double. May I proceed?"

35. Service without excessive delays

What: Call flows smoothly; tasks handled efficiently.

Why: Signals mastery and respect for guest's time.

How: Use templates, have rate grids ready, offer call-back if delays.

Example: "Please give me a moment to record your details. Thank you for your patience."
